Celebrating Wedding Online

( product design / web design)

The pandemic has changed the way we live and also how we hold a wedding. Despite the harsh situation, my friend Chin-Chen still remains her plan to hold the wedding.

But due to the timing, her family and friends couldn't fly to Switzerland and celebrate such a big day. So, after talking over the reality we faced, as her bridesmaid, I am in charge of helping her develop an online wedding invitation that allows her beloved person to participate even in the distance.

▍duration: Mar-May.2021

goal

＞ create the look of the online invitation site.

＞ interview with the guests and find the best platform to gather everyone

process

▍Design Concept

To understand what kind of feeling she would like to present in the invitation, I asked her to collect some inspiration photos and made a mood board. According to it and discussion, we decided to create a Nordic forest style and host it on WordPress.

▍Choose the platform

After put the webpage online within a month. Chin-Chen sent out the invitation link, and I assisted in collecting the list of participants who prefer to join virtually.

Then I sent out a survey to them to understand which online gathering platform they are familiar with. The most popular platform among these 48 people was Facebook.

However, with the concern that new people may want to join and would reach the limitation of Messenger and Line might be a bit confusing for foreigners to register, we choose to stream the ceremony with Google Meet.

Besides the notification sent by Google, we also create a simple infographic to instruct the guests to join the event even though they have no experience in using Google Meet before.

outcome

Even though I have very limited time to do this project besides my daytime work, and I feel there are spaces for me to improve web design, the outcome still makes me happy, especially when seeing Chin-Chen's parents sweeping their tears on face while watching their beloved daughter getting married from Google Meet.
