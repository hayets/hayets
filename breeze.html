Breeze Daily

( product design / web design / branding )

Breeze Group is a luxury department store in Taiwan. With an ambition to expand their business from offline to online, they launched two online branches. One is their online marketplace named Breeze Online, and the other is Breeze Daily. Its aims are reshaping the brand image and have the voice of fashion in the young generation.

▍duration: March.2020-June.2021

problems

1- Unable To Customize & Use The Site

They bought a website build on WordPress from the contract IT company. However, the editorial board is unfamiliar with the system and faces difficulty while using it.

2- the company gives a distant feeling to the young customer

The company's brick-and-mortar store is luxury-oriented and left an unaffordable, distant impression on the young generation, which is the group of people they want to win over.

goals

＞ create the look of the website with the demand and help the editorial board understanding site features.

＞ decrease the commercial feeling while allowing readers to discover the product ,visit Breeze Online and track down the audience demographics.

＞ enhance the voice of Breeze Group on fashion and consumer goods online.

process

My role is web designer and social media planner. In the first three months, I have meetings with the editorial board nearly everyday to discuss the features, the layout, and the look they wish.

In the meeting, I would present the sample outlook and ask for their feedback. After that, the final designs were decided by consensus with the whole editorial team.

▍publish a post in 15 min: a guide to make backend panel easy to use

To understand the difficulty the editors were facing, I wrote a sample article, which includes most features that will be used often, and asked them to make it live to the site. I would instruct and observed aside. After they finished, I would do a quick survey to understand which part confused them the most.

The interview and observation show that adjusting the image, setting columns, and embedding external code/link are the hardest part for the editors. Based on it, I break down the steps and visualize them in the operation manual.

▍links matter

''the devil is in the links'' that's the pitch for me to suggest editor-in-chief do UTM tracking even it might be a bit troublesome for them to learn and use at the beginning. This simple action has favored the site after traffic coming in.

Tracking where the readers came can place the advertisement more accurately and grasp the audience demographics. We also placed the tracking code in the link when conducting the campaign with other media to see the performance and decide further cooperation.

▍and also the buttons

While informing the reader of Breeze Group's online marketplace without overpromotion, the editorial board and I brainstormed the idea of writing styling articles and putting a button under the product, which can be found in Breeze Online. And I am in charge of design the button.

At first, I designed 16 different buttons for the leader of BO and BD to choose from. They narrowed it down to 2 buttons but with disagreement between their concerns:

＞ Breeze Daily: small underline button, for a smoother reading experience
＞ Breeze Online: bid button, to attract the attention of the readers

There's no problem with both buttons on the web page since I made both of them can be hover over and present the clickable appearance. But when it comes to the mobile page, the small underline button will be less identifiable as the studies by the MIT Touch Lab suggest that the button shouldn't be smaller than 10mm x 10mm due to the average size of fingertips.

The research I did also confirm this study. I sent the comparison question about these two buttons to 100 office colleagues, which shows that out of the valid answers, the big button has almost three times the voting rates than the underline one.

And we made the big button to be the CTA button in the end. The button has more than 5k+ CTR since we embed it in the articles.

▍branding & SEO

In addition, I assisted in managing social media, analyzing audiences, placing online advertisements, and improving SEO for the site.

-Branding

＞ Social Media: by interviewing and cooperate with youg celebrities, the editorial board aimed to expan support to create social media contents by image retourching and video editing.
＞Cooperate With Other Media: co-work with Yahoo Style, Elle, and UDN Style on the mutual campaign.

-SEO

By improving SEO for the site and articles, google search bring over 506K+ clicks to visit the site in 15 months and become the most significant viewing traffic for Breeze Daily.

＞ Keyword Research: For the article on the site, I would make a keyword list and try to put a specific word in it to make the article a relevant searching result on google.
＞ Weekly SEO Report: Each week, I would present the result of SEO improvement (the searching rank, CTR, and CVr) to the editorial board and share my keyword list in assisting them to develop the new contents.
